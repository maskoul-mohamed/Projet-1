Linus Torvalds

Computer programmer

Founder and coordinator of Linux, the Unix-like operating system.

  • Born: 28 December 1969 (age 51)

  • Nationality: Finnish (by birth) American

Biography

  • 1969

    born on December 28, 1969 in Helsinki.

  • 1983

    Richard Stallman created the Free Software Foundation (GNU project).

  • 1986

    Design of the Unix Operating System by Marice J. Bach was published. Minix period (1988-1991).

  • 1988

    Admitted to the University . The same year Minix emerged.

  • 1990

    Takes his first C programming class.

  • 1991

    The beginning of Linux development (1991-1992)

  • 1994

    WEB revolution started with Linux as one of the major beneficiaries. A very successful FreeBSD 1.1 was released.

  • 1996

    The asteroid 9793 Torvalds was named after him

  • 1997

    Torvalds received his master's degree (Laudatur Grade) from the Department of Computer Science at the University of Helsinki.

  • 1998

    He received an EFF Pioneer Award.

  • 1999

    He received honorary doctor status at Stockholm University.

  • 2000

    He received honorary doctor status at University of Helsinki.

  • 2001

    he shared the Takeda Award for Social/Economic Well-Being with Richard Stallman and Ken Sakamura.

  • 2004

    In the search for the 100 Greatest Finns of all time, voted in the summer of 2004, Torvalds placed 16th..

  • 2005

    Disappears for a weekend and emerges the following week with Git, the leading distributed version control system.

  • 2005

    Releases the first public version of Git, Version 0.99.

  • 2005

    In 2005 he appeared as one of "the best managers" in a survey by BusinessWeek.

  • 2006

    Time Magazine names him one of the revolutionary heroes of the past 60 years.

  • 2014

    Receives the IEEE Computer Society's Computer Pioneer Award.
